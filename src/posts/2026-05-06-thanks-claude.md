I use Claude Code at work every day. I tend to like it. The stuff that I hate doing manually (searching for usages of a specific endpoint by other services, understanding potential code paths, digging deep into Spring's particularities) becomes a lot more enjoyable because I can let it loose without a second thought. I recognize that letting a non-deterministic tool handle these things brings the potential for error, but I personally don't believe that these kinds of tasks necessitate determinism. It's a tradeoff, for sure, but most things in engineering are to a degree. 



With all that said, it's a weird thing to feel emotion of any kind when interacting with a chatbot. In the past, getting frustrated while programming was usually a one-sided event, but now, it's really odd to harbor frustration for something that presents itself as having human qualities. In principle, I have problems with anthropomorphizing chatbots, but like most people, I also can't help it. We've been given an interface that requires us to interact with it this way.
